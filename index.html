A letter

Why we make footbags.

There is a parking lot in Houston where five of us meet on Saturdays. We have been doing this for years. Nobody is paid, nobody is famous, nobody scores anything. We just kick a small handmade ball back and forth, and somehow it has become the most spiritually honest hour of my week.

There is no clock in the circle. There is no winning. There is only the present moment, the next kick, and the people standing across from you. If you drop the bag, you pick it up. If your friend drops it, you don't say anything about it. The next kick is always more important than the last one.

It took me a long time to realize that this is what discipleship feels like. Not the polished version with the highlight reel and the worship band, but the quiet version. The faithful version. The version that shows up on Saturday whether anyone is watching or not.

AMBORN footbags are made for that circle. Twenty panels of Amara suede or hand-crocheted cotton, depending on the line. Twelve dollars each. Built to take ten thousand kicks and then ten thousand more. Each one carries a verse, not as decoration but as declaration.

The Dirt Bag Line is for the player who is being formed. Hardcover, fast, made for the asphalt. The Grace Line is for the player who is being given. Soft, slow, made for the pause between kicks. You will know which one is yours.

— The founder · Houston, TX
